# CRY - Cryptography

## CRY-01 Policy on the use of encryption methods and key management
Policies and instructions containing technical and organizational measures for encryption procedures and key management are documented, communicated, and provided in accordance with SP-01, describing the following aspects:

- The use of strong encryption procedures and secure network protocols that correspond to the state of the art
- Risk-based regulations for the use of encryption that are aligned with information classification schemes and take into account the communication channel as well as the type, strength, and quality of the encryption
- Requirements for the secure generation, storage, archiving, retrieval, distribution, revocation, and deletion of keys
- Consideration of relevant legal and regulatory obligations and requirements

## CRY-02 Encryption of data during transmission (transport encryption)
The cloud provider has established procedures and technical measures for strong encryption and authentication for the transmission of cloud customers' data over public networks.

## CRY-03 Encryption of sensitive data during storage
The cloud provider has established procedures and technical measures for encrypting cloud customers' data during storage. The private keys used for encryption are known exclusively to the cloud customer in accordance with applicable legal and regulatory obligations and requirements. Exceptions follow a regulated procedure. The procedures for using private keys, including any exceptions, must be contractually agreed upon with the cloud customer.

## CRY-04 Secure key management
The procedures and technical measures for the secure management of keys under the cloud provider's responsibility include at least the following aspects:

- Key generation for different cryptographic systems and applications
- Issuing and obtaining public key certificates
- Provisioning and activation of keys
- Secure storage of keys (separation of the key management system from the application and middleware levels), including a description of how authorized users gain access
- Changing or updating cryptographic keys, including policies that specify the conditions and method under which changes or updates are to be implemented
- Handling compromised keys
- Revocation and deletion of keys
- If pre-shared keys are used, the specifics regarding the secure use of this procedure are listed separately

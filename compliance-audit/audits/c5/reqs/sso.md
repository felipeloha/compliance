# SSO - Supplier and Service Provider Management

## SSO-01 Guidelines and instructions for controlling and monitoring third parties
Policies and instructions for the management and monitoring of third parties (e.g., service providers or suppliers) whose services contribute to the provision of the cloud service are documented, communicated, and provided in accordance with SP-01 with regard to the following aspects:

- Requirements for assessing the risks resulting from the procurement of third-party services
- Requirements for classifying third parties based on a risk assessment by the cloud provider and determining whether they are subcontractors
- Information security requirements for the processing, storage, or transmission of information by third parties, which are based on recognized industry standards
- Requirements for raising awareness and training of personnel in information security
- Applicable legal and regulatory requirements
- Requirements for dealing with vulnerabilities, security incidents, and disruptions
- Requirements for contractually agreeing on these requirements
- Requirements for monitoring these requirements
- Requirements for communicating these requirements to service providers employed by the third parties, insofar as the services of these service providers also contribute to the provision of the cloud service

## SSO-02 Risk assessment of service providers and suppliers
Service providers and suppliers of the cloud provider are subject to a risk assessment in accordance with the policies and instructions for managing and monitoring third parties before they contribute to the provision of the cloud service. The adequacy of the risk assessment is reviewed regularly, at least annually, by qualified personnel of the cloud provider during the service procurement period.

The risk assessment includes the identification, analysis, evaluation, treatment, and documentation of risks related to the following aspects:

- The need to protect the information with regard to the confidentiality, integrity, availability, and authenticity that is processed, stored, or transmitted by the third party
- The impact of a breach of the protection requirements on the provision of the cloud service
- The cloud provider's dependence on the service provider or supplier with regard to the scope, complexity, and uniqueness of the service purchased, including consideration of possible alternatives

## SSO-03 Directory of service providers and suppliers
The cloud provider registers service providers and suppliers who contribute to the provision of the cloud service. The following information must be maintained:

- Company name
- Address
- Locations where data is processed and stored
- Responsible contact person at the service provider/supplier
- Responsible contact person at the cloud provider
- Description of the service
- Classification based on the risk assessment
- Start of service provision
- Evidence of compliance with the contractually agreed requirements

The information in the directory is reviewed at least annually for completeness, accuracy, and validity.

## SSO-04 Monitoring compliance with requirements
The cloud provider monitors compliance with information security requirements and applicable legal and regulatory requirements in accordance with the policies and instructions for managing and monitoring third parties.

Monitoring includes a regular review of the following documentation, to the extent that it is required to be provided by the third parties in accordance with the contractual agreements:

- Reports on the quality of service provision
- Certificates confirming the conformity of management systems with international standards
- Reports from independent third parties on the adequacy and effectiveness of their service-related internal control systems
- Records of how third parties handle vulnerabilities, security incidents, and disruptions

The frequency of monitoring corresponds to the classification of the third parties based on the cloud provider's risk assessment (see SSO-02). The results of the monitoring are incorporated into the review of the risk assessment.

Identified violations and deviations are analyzed, evaluated, and addressed in accordance with the risk management process (see OIS-07).

## SSO-05 Exit strategy for receiving benefits
The cloud provider has defined and documented exit strategies for the procurement of services where the risk assessment of the service providers and suppliers revealed a very high level of dependency regarding the scope, complexity, and uniqueness of the purchased service.

The exit strategies are aligned with the business continuity plans and include the following aspects:

- Analysis of the potential costs, impacts, resources, and timing implications of transitioning a purchased service to an alternative service provider or supplier
- Definition and allocation of roles, responsibilities, and sufficient resources to carry out the transition activities
- Definition of success criteria for the transition
- Definition of indicators for monitoring service delivery, which should initiate the exit from the service in the event of unacceptable results

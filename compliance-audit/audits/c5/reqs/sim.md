# SIM - Security Incident Management

## SIM-01 Policy for handling security incidents
Policies and instructions containing technical and organizational measures are documented, communicated, and made available in accordance with SP-01 to ensure a rapid, effective, and orderly response to all known security incidents.

The cloud provider defines guidelines for the classification, prioritization, and escalation of security incidents and creates interfaces to incident management and business continuity management.

In addition, the cloud provider has established a Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT) to contribute to the coordinated resolution of specific security incidents.

Customers affected by security incidents are informed promptly and appropriately.

## SIM-02 Handling security incidents
Qualified personnel of the cloud provider, if necessary together with external security service providers, carry out classifications, prioritizations and root cause analyses for events that could constitute a security incident.

## SIM-03 Documentation and reporting of security incidents
After a security incident has been resolved, the solution is documented in accordance with the contractual agreements and the report is sent to affected customers for final information or, if necessary, as confirmation.

## SIM-04 Obligation of users to report security incidents to a central location
The cloud provider informs employees and external business partners of their obligations. If necessary, they agree or contractually commit to promptly reporting all security events of which they become aware and which are directly related to the cloud service provided by the cloud provider to a previously designated central location within the cloud provider.

The cloud provider communicates that false reports of events that subsequently turn out not to be incidents will not result in negative consequences.

## SIM-05 Evaluation and learning process
Mechanisms are in place to measure and monitor the nature and extent of security incidents, as well as to report them to supporting agencies. The information obtained from the evaluation is used to identify recurring or high-impact incidents and to determine the need for enhanced security measures.

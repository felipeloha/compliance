# BCM - Business Continuity Management

## BCM-01 Responsibility by the company management
The cloud provider's top management is designated as the process owner of continuity and emergency management and is responsible for establishing the process within the organization and adhering to the guidelines. They must ensure that sufficient resources are provided for an effective process.

People in the company's management and other relevant leadership positions demonstrate leadership and commitment to this topic, for example, by requesting or encouraging employees to actively contribute to the effectiveness of continuity and emergency management.

## BCM-02 Business Impact Analysis Policies and Procedures
Policies and instructions for determining the impact of any disruptions to the cloud service or the company are documented, communicated, and made available in accordance with SP-01.

At least the following aspects are considered:

- Possible scenarios based on a risk analysis
- Identification of critical products and services
- Identification of dependencies, including processes, applications, business partners, and third parties
- Identification of threats to critical products and services
- Identification of impacts resulting from planned and unplanned disruptions and their change over time
- Determination of the maximum acceptable duration of disruptions
- Determination of recovery priorities
- Determination of time targets for the resumption of critical products and services within the maximum acceptable time period (RTO)
- Determination of time targets for the maximum acceptable time period in which data is lost and cannot be recovered (RPO)
- Estimation of the resources required for recovery

## BCM-03 Business continuity planning
Based on the business impact analysis, a unified framework for business continuity planning and the business plan is introduced, documented, and applied to ensure consistency across all plans. Planning is based on established standards, which is clearly documented in a Statement of Applicability.

Business continuity plans and emergency plans consider the following aspects:

- Defined purpose and scope, taking into account relevant dependencies
- Accessibility and understandability of the plans for those who are to act on them
- Ownership by at least one designated individual responsible for review, updating, and approval
- Defined communication channels, roles, and responsibilities, including customer notification
- Recovery procedures, manual workarounds, and reference information
- Methods for implementing the plans
- Continuous improvement process for the plans
- Interfaces to security incident management

## BCM-04 Verification, updating and testing of business continuity
The business impact analysis, as well as the business continuity and emergency plans, are reviewed, updated, and tested regularly (at least annually) or after significant organizational or environmental changes. Tests involve affected customers (tenants) and relevant third parties. The tests are documented, and the results are considered for future business continuity measures.

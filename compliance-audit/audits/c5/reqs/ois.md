# OIS - Organizational Information Security

## OIS-01 Information Security Management System (ISMS)
The cloud provider operates an information security management system (ISMS) in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001. The scope of the ISMS includes the cloud provider's organizational units, locations, and procedures for providing the cloud service.

The measures for establishing, implementing, maintaining, and continuously improving the ISMS are documented.

The documentation includes:

- Scope of the ISMS (Section 4.3 of ISO/IEC 27001)
- Statement of applicability (Section 6.1.3)
- Results of the most recent management review (Section 9.3)

## OIS-02 Information security guideline
The cloud provider's top management has adopted an information security policy and communicated it to internal and external employees, as well as cloud customers. The policy describes:

- The importance of information security, derived from the cloud customers' information security-related requirements
- The security objectives and the desired security level, derived from the cloud provider's business objectives and tasks
- The key aspects of the security strategy for achieving the set security objectives
- The organizational structure for information security within the scope of the ISMS

## OIS-03 Interfaces and dependencies
Interfaces and dependencies between activities for providing the cloud service performed by the cloud provider itself and activities performed by third parties are documented and communicated.

This includes handling the following events:

- Vulnerabilities
- Security incidents
- Disruptions

The type and scope of documentation are based on the information needs of the relevant personnel of the affected organizations in order to be able to carry out the activities appropriately.

Changes to interfaces and dependencies are communicated promptly so that the affected third parties can respond appropriately with organizational and technical measures before they take effect.

## OIS-04 Separation of duties
Conflicting tasks and areas of responsibility are separated based on a risk assessment in accordance with OIS-06 to reduce the risk of unauthorized or unintentional changes or misuse of cloud customers' data processed, stored, or transmitted in the cloud service.

The risk assessment covers the following areas, insofar as they are applicable to the provision of the cloud service and are within the responsibility of the cloud provider:

- Management of rights profiles, approval and assignment of access authorizations (see IDM-01)
- Development, testing, and release of changes (see DEV-01)
- Operation of system components

If separation cannot be established for organizational or technical reasons, measures are in place to monitor activities in order to detect unauthorized or unintentional changes and misuse and to initiate appropriate countermeasures.

## OIS-05 Contact with relevant authorities and interest groups
The cloud provider maintains contact with relevant authorities and ministries to obtain information about current vulnerabilities and threats. This information is incorporated into the procedures for managing risks (see OIS-06) and vulnerabilities (see OPS-19).

## OIS-06 Guidelines for dealing with risks
Guidelines and instructions for the risk management process are documented, communicated, and provided in accordance with SP-01 regarding the following aspects:

- Identification of risks related to the loss of confidentiality, integrity, availability, and authenticity of information within the scope of the ISMS and assignment of risk owners
- Analysis of the probabilities of occurrence and impact upon occurrence, and determination of the risk level
- Evaluation of the risk analysis based on defined criteria for risk acceptance and prioritization of treatment
- Treatment of risks through measures, including approval of measures and acceptance of residual risks by risk owners
- Documentation of the activities for applying the process to obtain consistent, valid, and comparable results when applied repeatedly

## OIS-07 Application of the risk management procedure
The cloud provider applies the risk management process on an ad hoc basis, but at least annually. When identifying risks, the following aspects are considered, provided they are applicable to the cloud service provided and are within the cloud provider's area of responsibility: physical and environmental risks, organizational risks, personnel risks, technical risks, and risks arising from external parties.

# HR - Human Resources

## HR-01 Verification of qualifications and trustworthiness
The qualifications and trustworthiness of all internal and external employees of the cloud provider with access to cloud customer data or system components under the cloud provider's responsibility, who are responsible for providing the cloud service in the production environment, are reviewed by the cloud provider prior to employment in accordance with local laws and regulations. To the extent legally permissible, the review includes the following:

- Verification of the individual by ID card
- Verification of the resume
- Verification of academic titles and degrees
- Certificate of good conduct or national equivalents
- Assessment of the risk of blackmail

## HR-02 Employment and contractual conditions
The cloud provider's internal and external employees are required by their employment and contractual terms and conditions to comply with applicable policies and instructions related to information security.

Internal and external employees must demonstrate that they have read and understood the information security policy and the policies and instructions derived from it before being granted access to cloud customer data or system components under the cloud provider's responsibility that are used to provide the cloud service in the production environment.

## HR-03 Security training and awareness program
The cloud provider operates a targeted awareness and training program, which is regularly completed by all internal and external employees of the cloud provider. The program is regularly updated based on changes to policies and instructions as well as the current threat situation and covers the following aspects:

- Handling system components used to provide the cloud service in the production environment in accordance with applicable policies and instructions
- Handling cloud customer data in accordance with applicable policies and instructions
- Information about the current threat situation
- Proper behavior in the event of security incidents

## HR-04 Disciplinary process
In the event of violations of policies and instructions, disciplinary action is taken according to a defined process that includes the following aspects:

- Determining whether a violation has actually occurred
- Considering the nature and severity of the violation, as well as its impact

The cloud provider's internal and external employees are informed of possible disciplinary action.

The application of disciplinary action is documented appropriately.

## HR-05 Responsibilities upon termination or change of employment
Internal and external employees are verifiably informed about how long and which responsibilities arising from the policies and instructions relating to information security will remain in place even if their employment is terminated or changed.

## HR-06 Confidentiality agreements
The non-disclosure or confidentiality agreements to be concluded with the cloud provider's internal employees, external service providers, and suppliers are based on the requirements identified by the cloud provider for protecting confidential information and operational details.

The agreements must be concluded with external service providers and suppliers upon contract conclusion. Agreements must be concluded with the cloud provider's internal employees before authorization to access cloud customer data is granted.

The requirements must be documented and reviewed regularly (at least annually). If the review indicates that the requirements need to be adjusted, the non-disclosure or confidentiality agreements will be updated.

The cloud provider must inform the internal employees, external service providers, and suppliers of this and conclude the updated non-disclosure or confidentiality agreements with them.
